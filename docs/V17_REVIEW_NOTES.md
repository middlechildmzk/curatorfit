# CuratorFit V1.7 Review Notes

## Sprint title
CuratorFit V1.7 - SEO Content Engine + Trust Tools Hub

## What changed
- Added `/blog` article index and `/blog/[slug]` article pages.
- Added seeded P0 content library for playlist pitching, scam prevention, and AI music release guidance.
- Added `/guides` hub.
- Added `/ai-music` Suno/Udio/AI-assisted release hub.
- Added `/alternatives` and `/alternatives/[slug]` comparison pages.
- Added `/genres` and `/genres/[slug]` genre SEO pages.
- Added `/tools` hub.
- Added lightweight tools:
  - Playlist Pitch Generator
  - Spotify Playlist Scam Checker
  - AI Music Release Checklist
  - Playlist Submission Tracker
- Added trust CTAs inside articles.
- Added AI music disclaimer blocks.
- Added Article JSON-LD schema on article pages.
- Updated nav/footer with Blog, Tools, AI Music, Genres, and Alternatives.

## Strategic purpose
V1.7 turns CuratorFit from a SaaS shell into a traffic engine. The highest-leverage content lanes are:
1. Honest Spotify playlist pitching.
2. Spotify playlist scam prevention.
3. AI music release guidance for Suno/Udio creators.
4. Comparison/alternatives pages.
5. Genre-specific playlist pitching.
6. Free tools that lead into the campaign tracker.

## Review focus
- Does the content structure support SEO?
- Do CTAs naturally point into CuratorFit campaigns, targets, and tools?
- Is the AI music language cautious enough?
- Are there any risky claims around playlist placement, streams, rights, or distributor rules?
- Which tool should become fully functional first?
